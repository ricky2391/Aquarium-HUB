Morning
9:00 AM

Small frozen or pellet meal

Feed: Thawed mysis, calanus, enriched brine, or quality marine pellets.

Amount: Start with roughly ¼ frozen cube total or a small pinch of pellets. Add in two small portions; stop when food is no longer being eaten immediately.

For: Firefish, clowns, chromis, both tangs, and opportunistic shrimp.

Midday
Grazing

Nori for both tangs

Feed: Plain dried marine algae on a clip or secured to a rock.

Amount: Begin with about a 2 × 3 inch sheet shared by the Yellow Tang and Tomini Tang; adjust so most is consumed during the day.

For: Continuous herbivorous grazing, body condition, and reduced picking at inappropriate surfaces.

Evening
6:00 PM

Varied protein meal

Feed: Rotate mysis, finely chopped seafood blend, calanus, or pellets rather than relying on one food.

Amount: Another small meal—approximately ¼ frozen cube total or the pellet equivalent, divided so shy firefish receive food.

For: Maintaining weight and giving the firefish and chromis a second opportunity to feed.

Weekly
Optional

Target-feed the hammer and octospawn

Feed: One or two tiny mysis pieces or a small amount of suitable coral food after the fish meal.

Amount: Only what each coral can capture without food sitting on the tissue.

For: Supplemental nutrition; not required if the corals are healthy and receive adequate light and dissolved nutrients.

Feeding rules

  • Thaw frozen food in a small cup and avoid pouring excess packing liquid into the aquarium.
  • Spread food across the tank so the four firefish and the chromis are not outcompeted.
  • Watch body shape and appetite. Increase or reduce portions gradually—not from a single feeding.
  • Remove uneaten nori before it breaks apart, and reduce food if nitrate or phosphate rises persistently.
  • Hermits and shrimp usually scavenge leftovers; dedicated feeding is only needed when little food reaches them.

Saltwater mixing guide

Tropic Marin Pro-Reef mixing and storage steps
Mixing guide⌄
  1. Measure RODI water.Add the planned number of gallons to a clean, food-safe mixing container. Leave enough room for circulation and displacement.
  2. Start circulation.Run a clean powerhead so the entire container is moving. Add a heater when room temperature is far from the aquarium temperature.
  3. Add the calculated salt gradually.Use the cup estimate from the water-change logger as a starting amount. Add salt to moving water—not water onto a pile of dry salt.
  4. Mix until completely clear.Confirm no salt remains on the bottom or walls. Avoid excessive aeration or very long heated storage.
  5. Measure salinity and temperature.Use the calibrated Hanna salinity tester. Adjust with small amounts of salt or RODI water until the new water matches the aquarium.
  6. Use or store correctly.For immediate use, keep the pump running until the change. For storage, use a clean covered container, keep contaminants out, and label the mix date and salinity.
Mixed saltwater storage

Store covered in a clean container. Keep the lid secure but do not use a container that previously held chemicals. Recheck salinity and temperature before use.

Dry salt storage

Seal the bucket or bag tightly and keep it cool and dry. Use a completely dry scoop so moisture does not form hard clumps or alter the mixture.

Mixing equipment

After use, rinse the container, pump, heater, tubing, and measuring cup. Periodically remove mineral residue with a reef-safe citric-acid cleaning solution, then rinse thoroughly.
